## Problem section

### Heading
Most voice-agent setups break down after the demo.

### Body copy
It is easy to launch a voice assistant.

It is much harder to manage the real operating layer around it:
- client accounts
- lead activity
- call outcomes
- transcripts
- blast campaigns
- admin controls
- support workflows
- what the client can actually see and change

Most teams end up with a messy stack of dashboards, transcripts, spreadsheets, prompts, and support messages.

The result is poor visibility, weak client experience, and a platform that feels impressive in a pitch but frustrating in production.

## Feature blocks

### 1. Admin control plane
Create and manage client accounts, deploy assistant stacks, control rollout defaults, and maintain a cleaner operating layer across tenants.

### 2. Client-facing portal
Give clients a real dashboard where they can review activity, see call outcomes, inspect transcripts, and manage approved settings without needing constant manual support.

### 3. Lead and activity visibility
Track what happened, not just that something happened. Surface leads, interactions, outcomes, and transcript history in a way clients can actually use.

### 4. Inbound + outbound voice workflows
Support both sides of the operation: inbound answering and routing, plus outbound campaigns, test calls, and list-based activity.

### 5. Production-oriented multi-tenant foundation
Built around org isolation, auth, and RLS-ready data boundaries so the platform can behave like a real SaaS instead of an improvised internal tool.

## FAQ section

### Is this just for inbound agents?
No. The platform is designed for both inbound and outbound voice operations.

### Can clients log in and see their own data?
Yes. The system is built around multi-tenant/client-facing access patterns with role and data boundaries in mind.

### Can this support transcripts and lead activity?
Yes. That is one of the core commercial strengths of the platform: clients need visibility into what happened, what leads came in, and how calls performed.

### Is this production-ready?
The platform already has a strong deployed foundation across Vercel, Railway, Supabase, and Vapi. The current focus is product polish, client-facing quality, and making the experience tighter and more sellable.

### Is this self-serve SaaS yet?
Not the way it should be marketed today. Right now the strongest move is a guided walkthrough / implementation model, with the product continuing to mature into a stronger client-facing platform.
